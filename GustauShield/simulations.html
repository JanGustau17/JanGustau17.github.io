Step 1: Fake Identity Presented

An attacker attempts to pass a facial recognition login by using a high-resolution photo of the real user’s face.

[They hold up a glossy photo to the camera, hoping to fool the system.]

Spoofing a biometric

Step 2: System Scanning Face

The security system scans the face. At first glance, it matches the authorized person's photo on record. It is on the verge of granting access.

[The attacker smirks, thinking this simple trick is working.]

Step 3: GustauShield Spots a Fake

GustauShield's vision AI performs a liveness check – it notices no blink, no natural skin texture or depth. This face is likely a photo!

[ALERT: "Face Liveness Failed – Access Blocked"]

Step 4: Access Denied

The system refuses to unlock. The attacker’s spoof attempt fails – the door stays closed (or the account remains locked).

[Security is alerted to a possible spoofing attempt. The attacker is left outside, caught on camera.]

Step 5: Spoof Stopped – Lessons Learned

  • Biometric systems should use liveness detection (e.g., ask for a blink or movement) to thwart imposters.
  • Never assume a static photo or video replay is sufficient ID; always layer with PINs or security questions for critical access.
  • GustauShield's AI can detect subtle differences between a live person and a fake image (lighting, eye reflections, etc.) to protect your accounts.
Result: The fake face is exposed, and the attacker gains no access.
